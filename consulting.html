Two ways to work together

Solar and renewable energy planning

For anyone staring at a solar quote, an aging gas furnace, or a flat roof and wondering what to actually do with it. I work the problem from your side of the table.

  • Site and roof assessment. Orientation, shading, structural questions worth asking before anyone drills.
  • Production and payback modeling. Real numbers for your roof and your bills, not brochure numbers.
  • Quote and bid review. Side-by-side breakdown of installer proposals before you sign anything.
  • Whole-building roadmap. Panels, heat pumps, water heating, EV charging, batteries, and what order to do them in.
  • Incentive navigation. The rules have changed a lot lately. Knowing what's actually on the table right now is part of the job.

Good for: homeowners, condo and HOA boards, congregations, small commercial

Climate and clean energy education

Energy is the rare subject where the classroom itself can be the lesson. I help schools and community groups teach climate and renewables with real buildings and real data instead of worksheets.

  • Workshops and talks. For classrooms, assemblies, PTAs, scout troops, and library programs.
  • Building-as-textbook design. Turning energy dashboards and rooftop arrays into math and science lessons.
  • Teacher support. Curriculum consulting and professional development that fits real class time.
  • Green team coaching. Helping student energy patrols and recycling teams actually move the meter.
  • Career pathway sessions. What clean energy jobs look like and how students get there.

Good for: schools and districts, teachers, PTAs, youth groups, libraries

How does it work?

Intro call

Thirty minutes, free! You describe the building or the classroom, I tell you honestly whether I can help.

Site visit or scoping

A walk-through of the roof, the mechanical room, or the syllabus. We define what done looks like.

The plan

A written roadmap with numbers, options, and sequencing. Yours to keep whether or not we go further.

Backup while you build

Bid reviews, installer questions, dashboard setup, or the first workshop delivered. I stay until it works.

Worth a call, or not

Consulting only works when it's honest, so here's the honest version.

Probably a good fit

  • You have installer quotes and want an independent read before signing
  • You sit on a condo, HOA, or congregation board weighing a solar or electrification project
  • You're a teacher or administrator who wants energy taught with real data
  • You want a multi-year plan, not a one-off purchase

Probably not

  • You need someone to sell or install equipment. I don't sell anything, which is the point
  • You need emergency roof or HVAC repair, that's a contractor call
  • You want a rubber stamp on a decision already made
  • Utility-scale projects, that's a different kind of firm

The guy behind the plan

Tanner lives in Rosslyn and spends his working days managing energy for Arlington's public school buildings, some of the most closely watched high-performance buildings in the country. Net zero schools, rooftop arrays, geothermal fields, live dashboards: he has run the numbers on all of it at building scale.

Prime Green consulting is that same discipline pointed at your roof, your board meeting, or your classroom. The advice is independent. He doesn't sell panels, doesn't take referral fees, and will tell you if solar is the wrong move for your site, because sometimes it is.
